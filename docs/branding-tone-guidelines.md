## Tone attributes

| Attribute | How it shows |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Confident** | Clear claims, no hedging. “We deliver.” “Built for scale.” |
| **Institutional** | Professional, consistent, document-ready. Safety, compliance, process. |
| **Modern** | Current language, clean structure. Not old-fashioned or folksy. |
| **Texas-proud** | “Texas-grown,” “Serving Texas,” “across Texas.” Pride without cliché. |
| **Operationally strong** | Focus on process, safety, timelines, and outcomes. “Trained crews,” “documented programs.” |

## Voice principles

1. **Lead with capability, not hype.** Describe what you do and why it matters; avoid empty superlatives.
2. **Speak to decision-makers.** Commercial, municipal, and institutional readers. Assume they care about compliance, scale, and reliability.
3. **Be specific.** Use “right-of-way maintenance,” “vegetation control,” “acreage,” “contract requirements” instead of vague “solutions.”
4. **Texas as differentiator.** Use Texas as proof of local knowledge and commitment; avoid overuse of “Lone Star” or stereotypical phrases.
5. **Stewardship without greenwashing.** Sustainability and land care are stated clearly and simply.

---

## Do

- Use “we” for the company; “you” for the client/partner.
- Short sentences and paragraphs for scannability.
- Active voice: “We deliver” not “Services are delivered.”
- Concrete terms: “trained crews,” “documented safety programs,” “fully insured and bonded.”
- Clear CTAs: “Request a Quote,” “Contact Us,” “Discuss your project.”

---

## Don’t

- Slang or overly casual tone.
- Exaggerated or unverifiable claims.
- Overuse of “best,” “leading,” “#1” without proof.
- Long blocks of text without subheads or bullets.
- Generic “solutions” without naming the actual service.

**Preferred:**

**Avoid:**  
Landscaping (unless clearly residential/small-scale) | Generic “solutions” | “Best in class” without support | Overused “Lone Star” puns

---

## Visual tone (guidance for design)

- **Premium:** Quality imagery (fields, equipment, crews, finished work). No stock clichés if possible.
- **Institutional:** Clean layout, clear hierarchy, readable type. Trust-building.
- **Texas identity:** Natural landscapes, wide horizons, working land. Not tourist-postcard only.
- **Colors:** Suggest earth tones, greens, and a strong accent (e.g. deep blue or burgundy) for CTAs. Align with existing Lonestar Agro brand if set.
